An Oriental Classic

David's Orange Chicken

This recipe is adapted from "Chinese Orange Chicken," written by Melissa Stadler, and found on https://www.modernhoney.com/chinese-orange-chicken/

Ingredients

  • 4 Boneless Skinless Chicken Breasts
  • 3 Eggs whisked
  • 1/3 cup Cornstarch
  • 1/3 cup Flour
  • 1 tbs Salt
  • Oil for frying
  • 1 cup Orange Juice
  • 2 Tablespoons Rice Vinegar or White Vinegar
  • 2 Tablespoons Soy Sauce use tamari for a gluten-free dish
  • 1/4 teaspoon Ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon Garlic Powder or 2 garlic cloves, finely diced
  • 1/2 teaspoon Red Chili Flakes
  • Orange Zest from 1 orange
  • 1 Tablespoon Cornstarch

Equipment

  • medium pot
  • heavy-bottomed pot
  • thermometer
  • shallow dish
  • whisk
  • serving plate
  • paper towels
  • cup
  • teaspoon
  • tablespoon
  • fork
  • knife
  • cutting board

Directions

  1. In a medium pot, add orange juice, sugar, vinegar, soy sauce, ginger, garlic, and red chili flakes. Heat for 3 minutes.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk 1 Tablespoon of cornstarch with 2 Tablespoons of water to form a paste. Add to orange sauce and whisk together. Continue to cook for 5 minutes, until the mixture begins to thicken. Once the sauce is thickened, remove from heat and add orange zest.
  3. Place flour and cornstarch in a shallow dish or pie plate. Add a generous pinch of salt. Stir.
  4. Whisk eggs in shallow dish.
  5. On a cutting board, cut chicken into bite-sized pieces.
  6. Dip chicken pieces in egg mixture and then flour mixture. Place on plate.
  7. Heat 2-3 inches of oil in a heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Using a thermometer, watch for it to reach 350 degrees.
  8. Working in batches, cook several chicken pieces at a time. Cook for 2 - 3 minutes, turning often until golden brown. Place chicken on a paper-towel-lined plate. Repeat.
  9. Toss chicken with orange sauce. You may reserve some of the sauce to place on rice. Serve it with a sprinkling of orange zest, if so desired.
